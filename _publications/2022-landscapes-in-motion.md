In this paper we argue for leveraging geospatial and environmental data and systems at the intersection of spatial, digital, and environmental approaches, with the goal of creating an open, accessible digital ecosystem that enables the discovery, use, and publication of digital environmental humanities data. We survey current innovations from the long-term and large-scale history of China, the ancient Mediterranean, and the globe, which collectively point toward a vision for a networked and cloud-based graph of historical environmental data.
